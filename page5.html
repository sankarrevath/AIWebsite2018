Timeline

The Advancement of AI Learning Technology

1950-Turing's Learning Machine

Alan Turing, reputed Computer Scientist, proposes a "learning machine" that could learn and become artificially intelligent.

1951-First Neural Network Machine

Marvin Minsky and Dean Edmonds build the first neural network machine, the SNARC.

1970-Backpropogation

Seppo Linnainmaa publishes the general method for automatic differentiation of discrete connected networks of nested differentiable functions.

1979-Stanford Cart

Students at Stanford University develop a cart that can navigate and avoid obstacles in a room.

1980-Neocognitron

Kunihiko Fukushima first publishes his work on the neocognitron, a type of artificial neural network. Neocognition inspires convolutional neural networks.

1981-Introduction to Explanation Based Learning

Gerald Dejong introduces Explanation Based Learning, where a computer algorithm analyses data and creates a general rule it can follow and discard unimportant data.

1997-IBM Deep Blue beats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue beats the current world champion at chess.

2002-Torch Machine Learning Library is released

Torch, the first software library for machine learning is released.

2014-Sibyl

Researchers from Google detail their work on Sibyl, a proprietary platform for massively parallel machine learning used internally by Google to make predictions about user behavior and provide recommendations.

2017-Google's AlphaZero beats current world Go and Chess champions.

Google's AlphaGo program becomes the first Computer Go program to beat an unhandiapped professional human player using a combination of machine learning and tree searching techniques. Later improved as AlphaGo Zero and then in 2017 generalized to Chess and more two-player games with AlphaZero.

The Future

The field of Artificial Intelligence is rapidly improving, with new projects in Transportation, with recreational projects such as the Google Car. In the fields of biotechnology, Yoky Matsuka of Nest believes that AI will become useful for people with amputated limbs, as the brain will be able to communicate with the robotic limb to give the patient more control. Finally, one of the most critical advances in AI is for robots to perform dangerous tasks that would normally put humans at terrible risk including bomb defusals, jobs that require toxic substances, intense heat and put severe stress on the human body.
